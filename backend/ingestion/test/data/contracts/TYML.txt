Tymlos (TYML)

Events that are not suspected to be related to Tymlos by the reporter are not considered adverse events and not reportable to Radius.

Adverse Event is defined as any untoward medical occurrence in a patient administered medicinal product, which does not necessarily have a causal relationship with the treatment.
An adverse event can therefore be any unfavorable and unintended sign (for example, an abnormal laboratory findings), symptom, or disease temporarily associated with the use of a medical product.
 A special situation concerning the product includes:
reports of off-label use with an associated Adverse Event
misuse
occupational exposure
medication error resulting from manufacturer labeling, packaging, or characteristics of the Product
falsified (counterfeit) product
use of the Product during pregnancy or breastfeeding
suspected transmission of an infectious agent
unintended beneficial effect
diversion (illegal, generally non-medical use)
